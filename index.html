xemantic.com

This website was born, because I wanted to adequately present all my work, spanning across variety of disciplines and expressed in diverse media. I express my self with the code, but it's not something you can easily perceive. So here is the wrapping.

Powered by bleeding edge technologies: webgl2, feedback loop synaesthetic shaders where the natural colors of the rainbow transforms into movement, new level of responsiveness in web design, golden ratio everywhere and extremely fast initial load - the graphics and animation are embedded into the source of this page, representing the ultimate conceptual compression. The whole thing is listed here, because it represents a significant project on it's own. Besides I love going meta. Look how cute it is as a recursive fractal. :)

So I also do websites. Maybe you need one? I can help building it or consult on techniques. I didn't want to copy Netflix navigation, but the comparison seems inevitable. Maybe it's a seed for an art-streaming service?

Emotional Robots

Imagine a swarm of robots who are curious, anxious, cute, obnoxious. Behaving like if they were experiencing affectionate states driving their agency.

I turned towards robotics during the time of pandemic, when the gig-economy collapsed, so did most of my other projects which live only with the audience perceiving them - esse est percipi. At least androids cannot be infected with bio-viruses. I am grateful that my friend Jacqueline Heer commissioned the first one of this breed of art-robots, to display her work. We will develop them much further.

I am building many of these robots currently, providing various experiences: insect-like movement among the crowd, machine poetry, gnostic encounters, video-skin. Check for updates soon. I open sourced the software developed for this purpose as we-are-the-robots project and I am happy to help with your emotional machines.

we-are-the-robots

Robotic software for artists - upcycling useful vacuum cleaners into useless (but maybe profound) experiences.

It's made out of building blocks, which can be easily assembled into new behavioral patterns. Although any kind of robot might benefit from this project, it is designed to deliver an experience for people interacting with the machine. See emotional robots for the experiences I am working on.

My name is Kazik Pogoda and I work as a creative technologist. A fancy phrase to say that instead of using Adobe products I write my own software. Achieving the aesthetics I desire requires writing a custom code. The practice is called creative coding, and the outcome is called generative art or generative design. I feel neither like an artist nor like a designer. I majored in philosophy and what I create is rather reflecting my philosophical believes on the evolution applied not only to the context of biology, but also human culture and our "symbiosis" with the machines weaving the new fabric of reality.

I enjoy speaking in their languages and I understand them well. But my love stays with humans. I love to collaborate, because it always brings additional humanistic narrative and dimension to my own work, when it is becoming part of some bigger story to tell. If you feel like using some of my generative aesthetics in your work - a performance, a show, a film, a website, a theatrical play, a sustainable product, ... - you name it, then feel free to contact me.

I started working with creative technologies very recently, even though it is something I was fascinated with as a teenager back in Poland, in the time when my home country was transforming from the communist to the capitalist economy. I've been working for years with software, as chief architect, lead developer and mentor for other programmers, in a very diverse international teams. I've produced software used by millions of human and non-human agents. You can find my full professional curriculum on LinkedIn.

But now I what to use code for a different purposes. I want to evoke affectionate states with the use of machines. Emotional generative art. Make you feel something when you perceive a product of an algorithm. Either thanks to purely aesthetic and synaesthetic experience, or thanks to the conceptual relevance, when the narrative accurately meditates on our post-human condition.
